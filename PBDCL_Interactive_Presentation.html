What is Problem-Based Digital Context Learning (PBDCL)?

4/16

Definition

A student-centered approach where learners engage with realistic, contextualized problems in a digital environment like simulations, virtual patients, and online platforms, to develop clinical reasoning and knowledge.

Key Principles of PBDCL

5/16
1
Problem as the Starting Point: Learning begins with a compelling clinical scenario
2
Student-Centered Learning: The tutor is a facilitator; students drive their own learning
3
Digital Immersion: Uses technology (VR, e-learning modules, video) to simulate reality
4
Collaboration in teams: Students work in teams, mirroring interdisciplinary healthcare
5
Reflection and critical analysis: Critical analysis of the learning process and outcomes is essential

The PBDCL Process

6/16
1
Present Problem: Introduce a digital clinical scenario
2
Identify Knowledge Gaps: What do we need to know to solve this?
3
Self-Directed Learning: Research using digital resources
4
Collaborate & Synthesize: Discuss findings and apply them to the problem
5
Present Solution & Reflect: Propose an intervention and reflect on the process

Interactive Activity: Build a PBDCL Program

9/16

Mission

Design a PBDCL curriculum for 'Post-Op Pain Management.'

Skill

Nursing Assessment and Intervention.

Tool

Sticky notes (virtual/physical).

Interactive Activity - Step 1 & 2

10/16

Step 1: Define the Problem Scenario.

"What is a realistic, challenging presentation for a chest pain patient in a KSA context?"

(e.g., "A 55-year-old Saudi male with a history of obesity presents with crushing chest pain.")

Step 2: Set Learning Objectives.

"What should a nurse be able to DO after solving this case?"

(e.g., "Prioritize initial nursing assessments," "Interpret a basic EKG," "Administer prescribed medications safely.")

Interactive Activity - Step 3 & 4 & 5

11/16

Step 3: Choose Digital Tools.

"What digital tools can students use to solve this?"

(e.g., "A video of the patient," "A simulated EKG app," "An online drug database," "A virtual 'huddle' on Teams.")

Step 4: Design the Assessment.

"How do we know they learned?"

(e.g., "Submit a digital nursing care plan," "Record a short video explaining their clinical reasoning.")

Step 5: Plan the Facilitation.

"What will the instructor do?"

(e.g., "Post the initial trigger," "Host the discussion forum," "Provide digital feedback on the care plan.")
